About Me

Hi, I'm Joey — a cartographer and conservationist based in the Pacific Northwest. I hold a B.S. in Wildlife Ecology and an M.S. in Cartography & GIS, both from the University of Wisconsin–Madison.

I specialize in creating visually engaging, data-driven maps that tell the stories of our natural world. My professional background blends hands-on environmental fieldwork with cartographic visualization and geospatial analysis, drawing on experience as a field researcher, naturalist, land steward, and GIS technician.

You can read more about my work experience here.

Outside of work, I love birding, wildlife photography, hiking, rugby, and traveling the world.
